The Complete Guide to Buying Handmade Gifts in the UK

8 April 2026  ·  Croi Concrete Journal  ·  All posts

The UK has a thriving handmade goods scene — from potters in Cornwall to concrete makers in Newcastle — yet most people still default to supermarket gift sets or Amazon wish lists. Here's why that's worth reconsidering, and how to do better.

Why Handmade Gifts Are Different

A handmade gift communicates something a factory-produced item can't: that someone chose it deliberately, from a real maker, knowing it was made by hand. The recipient can feel the difference — literally, in the weight and texture of the object, and figuratively, in what it implies about the thought that went into it.

Where to Find Handmade Gifts in the UK

Etsy remains the best single destination for UK handmade goods. Filter by location to support British makers. Craft markets — particularly Christmas markets in cities like Newcastle, Edinburgh, Bristol, and London — are excellent for finding makers you'd never discover online.

What Makes a Good Handmade Gift

The best handmade gifts are useful. Think about what the recipient actually uses daily: coasters on their coffee table, a planter for their desk, a pen holder in their office. These are gifts that become part of someone's everyday environment — quietly appreciated every day.

Concrete Homeware as a Gift

Handmade concrete homeware gifts sit in a sweet spot: genuinely unusual, practical, and durable. A set of concrete coasters or a small concrete planter makes a strong gift for housewarmings, birthdays, or Christmas.

The Honest Advice

Don't overthink it. Find a maker whose work you genuinely like, buy something you'd want yourself, and wrap it properly. The handmade provenance does the rest of the work.
